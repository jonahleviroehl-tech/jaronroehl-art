## Design Context

### Users
Art collectors, gallery curators, commission clients, and casual art appreciators visiting Jaron Röhl's portfolio. They arrive to evaluate the quality of his work, explore his artistic range (oil paintings, portraits, drawings, photography), and potentially purchase pieces or commission new work. The context is intentional browsing — they want to be immersed, not rushed.

### Brand Personality
**Contemplative. Refined. Subtly provocative.**
The work itself ranges from dark, moody realism (Koch portrait) to surrealist absurdism (Fischtube) — the site must hold space for both without favoring one register. The emotional goal is *quiet confidence*: the art commands attention, the site never competes with it.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Visual tone:** Museum-like, predominantly light (pastel white ~80-90%), with moments of bold drama (hero, transitions)
- **References:** The digital equivalent of a high-end Kunsthalle — generous white walls, precise hanging, deliberate pacing
- **Anti-references:** Busy Squarespace templates, social-media-grid aesthetics, anything that feels like a marketplace or thumbnail dump
- **Theme:** Light mode only. Warm white (#FAFAF8), not clinical white. Dark paintings create their own contrast against the pale background
- **Palette accents:** Muted sage green, warm beige/sand, soft grey-violet — used very sparingly (10-20%)

### Design Principles
1. **The art is the interface.** Every layout decision serves the artwork. White space is not empty — it is framing.
2. **Pacing over speed.** Transitions are slow, deliberate, museum-paced. The visitor walks, never scrolls frantically.
3. **Quiet baseline, bold punctuation.** Most of the experience is serene and contemplative, interrupted by occasional dramatic moments (hero entrance, artwork zoom).
4. **Materiality through restraint.** No decorative elements. Quality comes from typography, spacing, and motion — not ornament.
5. **Invisible craft.** The technical sophistication (GSAP animations, view transitions) should feel effortless and natural, never performative.
